A wheel loader is a type of heavy construction equipment equipped with large wheels and a front-mounted bucket, used primarily for scooping, transporting, and loading loose materials such as soil, gravel, sand, and debris. The loader’s versatility and mobility make it an essential machine on construction, mining, and agricultural sites.

Wheel loaders come in a variety of sizes:
  • Compact Wheel Loaders: Smaller models used for light construction, landscaping, or agriculture.
  • Mid-Sized Wheel Loaders: Versatile and used for general construction tasks, offering a balance between power and maneuverability.
  • Large Wheel Loaders: Heavy-duty models used in mining or quarry operations, capable of moving large amounts of material.

DGPS


DGPS(Differential Global Positioning System). It is an enhancement to the standard GPS (Global Positioning System) that provides improved accuracy by correcting the signal errors from GPS satellites. While a standard GPS can have errors in the range of 10 to 20 meters, DGPS can improve the accuracy to within 1 meter or less.

DREDGING


Dredging is the process of removing sediment, debris, or other material from the bottom of bodies of water such as rivers, lakes, harbors, or oceans.
Dredging come in several different types, including:

  • Hydraulic Dredging:- Uses high-pressure water jets to loosen sediment, which is then sucked up by a pipeline and transported elsewhere. This method is commonly used for deep-water dredging. Uses water jets and suction pumps to loosen and remove material from the seabed.
  • Mechanical Dredging:- Uses excavating equipment like clamshell buckets or draglines to physically scoop up the sediment. It’s typically used in shallow, smaller bodies of water.
